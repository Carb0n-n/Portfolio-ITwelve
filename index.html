About Valve

VALVe Corporation, also known as VALVe Software,
is an American video game developer, publisher, and digital distribution company founded in 1996 by the former Microsoft employee Gabe Newell (a.k.a Gaben).


Valve was founded in 1996 by the former Microsoft employees Gabe Newell and Mike Harrington. Their debut game, the first-person shooter (FPS) Half-Life (1998), was a critical and commercial success and had a lasting influence on the FPS genre.

VALVe has had a profound and lasting impact on the gaming industry through its innovations in game development, digital distribution, and player-driven ecosystems. The company not only created genre-defining titles like Half-Life, Half-Life, and Left 4 Dead, but also transformed how games are purchased and played with the launch of Steam, now the world’s .



VALVe pioneered community-centered features such as the Steam Workshop, empowering modders and creators to shape entire game economies. By pushing technological boundaries, championing user freedom, and setting new standards for online distribution, VALVe has helped shape modern PC gaming into what it is today.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where is Valve's headquarters located?

Valve's headquarters is situated in Bellevue, Washington, United States.

What is Valve's contribution to the gaming industry?

Valve has played a major role in popularising digital distribution via Steam, supporting eSports through Dota 2, and driving innovation through the Steam Workshop and community-created content.

How does Valve support the community of players and creators?

Valve provides the Steam Community, the Steam Workshop, and a modding system that allows creators to share their work with millions of players.

What is Steam and how does it work?

Steam is Valve's digital distribution platform that allows users to purchase, download, and play games, as well as interact with the community.

Does Valve have plans for the future?

Valve continually experiments with new technology, both in the fields of hardware and software. Project details are usually announced via their official channels.
